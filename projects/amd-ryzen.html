About the project

A YouTube trailer for AMD’s Ryzen Pinnacle Ridge CPUs, built around the idea of different users and creative worlds brought to life. I created two of the featured shots, both tracked and composited into stock footage — not easy without camera metadata. The first scene drops in towering, wireframe “War of the Worlds”-style robots onto a city street, designed, modelled and rigged by me in Blender and Cinema 4D. I recreated elements like the fence to resell the composite, then finished everything in After Effects. The second scene turns a busy city street into a holographic racing game, with custom ships designed in Blender, animated and rendered in C4D, and X-Particles driving the exhaust FX. Matching camera movement, integrating the ships into the architecture, and keeping them fast but readable made this one a real challenge, with plenty of roto and compositing work to ground it. I’m really pleased with how both shots turned out. I also animated all of the typographic overlays and holoram style UX for the whole film in After Effects.
